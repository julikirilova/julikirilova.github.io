In the UK, Christmas is usually snowless. So why do we still picture the ‘ideal’ Christmas as a white one?

It was so icy during Dickens’s early years that the River Thames froze in February of 1814. London celebrated its final Thames frost fair that year, which included people setting up tents on the ice for four days and an elephant being led across the river just below Blackfriars Bridge.
